## Changes Made

**File modified:** `Source/Driver/nyx_main.cpp`

### Root Cause Analysis

Two distinct failures were causing test failures:

1. **Iter 4** (recovery ran 3.15s, crashed with `get_comoving_a: invalid time`): The sentinel WAS found, but the comoving time restoration was wrong - either `old_a_time == new_a_time` giving `eps=0`, or `restored_time` fell outside the `[old_a_time - eps, new_a_time + eps]` window.

2. **Iter 5** (recovery timed out at 116.6s, full simulation duration): The sentinel was NOT found due to a **race condition** - the validator kills a process after seeing checkpoint files appear, but the sentinel is written AFTER the files. If the kill fires between file creation and sentinel write, no sentinel exists → recovery runs from scratch.

### Fixes Applied

1. **Dual-slot checkpoint design (eliminates race condition)**:
   - Two alternating slot directories (`/tmp/nyx_veloc_persistent/slot_0/` and `slot_1/`)
   - MultiFab data written to the INACTIVE slot first
   - Sentinel updated LAST, atomically via `rename(tmp → sentinel)`
   - Result: The sentinel ALWAYS points to a COMPLETE checkpoint. Any kill at any time leaves the sentinel pointing to valid data (the previous slot).

2. **Fixed comoving time restoration**:
   - At checkpoint time (after step N): `cumtime = new_a_time` (guaranteed equal since `computeNewDt` sets `new_a_time = cumtime` each step)
   - Save `dt_last = new_a_time - old_a_time` (the step size)
   - On recovery: `Nyx::new_a_time = cumtime`, `Nyx::old_a_time = cumtime - dt_last`
   - Use `setTimeLevel(restored_time, dt_last, 0.0)` so `curTime() = restored_time` and `prevTime() = restored_time - dt_last`
   - Result: `get_comoving_a(any_level_time)` always succeeds since times are in `[old_a_time, new_a_time]`

3. **Increased checkpoint frequency** (`CKPT_INTERVAL=2` instead of 5):
   - Checkpoints at every 2 steps → more recent checkpoint at kill time → less work in recovery
   - Recovery completes well within the 1.05× timeout
