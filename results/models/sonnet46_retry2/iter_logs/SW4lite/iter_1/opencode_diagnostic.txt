### 2. `src/EW.C` — VeloC checkpoint/restart in `EW::timesteploop`

**Include**: Added `#include <veloc.h>` after the other includes.

**Initialization block** (inserted after `beginCycle++`, before `copy_bcforcing_arrays_to_device()`):
- Allocates **fixed-pointer checkpoint buffers** `veloc_Uckpt[g]` and `veloc_Umckpt[g]` — these are heap-allocated arrays that never get swapped by `cycleSolutionArrays`, which only swaps the internal raw pointers inside the `Sarray` objects.
- A `double veloc_scalars[2]` holds `{t, currentTimeStep}`.
- Calls `VELOC_Init(MPI_COMM_WORLD, "veloc.cfg")`.
- Registers all buffers with `VELOC_Mem_protect`.
- Calls `VELOC_Restart_test("sw4lite", 0)` — if a checkpoint exists, calls `VELOC_Restart(...)` to restore the buffers, then `memcpy`s from the checkpoint buffers back into the live `U[g]` and `Um[g]` arrays, and updates `t` and `beginCycle` so the time loop resumes from the correct step.

**Checkpoint in the time loop** (inserted after `cycleSolutionArrays`):
- `memcpy`s current `U[g].c_ptr()` and `Um[g].c_ptr()` into the fixed checkpoint buffers (captures the post-cycling, post-update state).
- Updates `veloc_scalars` with current `t` and `currentTimeStep`.
- Calls `VELOC_Checkpoint("sw4lite", currentTimeStep)` — version number is the step count, which strictly increases.

**Cleanup** (at end of `timesteploop`):
- Frees checkpoint buffers.
- Calls `VELOC_Finalize(0)` before `MPI_Finalize` (which is in `main.C`).
