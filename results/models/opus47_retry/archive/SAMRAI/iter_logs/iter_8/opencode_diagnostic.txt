The previous attempt's failure mode was a segfault (exit 139) during recovery, occurring at 2.24s. The crash trace showed it was inside `mesh::GriddingAlgorithm::regridAllFinerLevels` → `regridFinerLevel_createAndInstallNewLevel` → `initializeLevelData` → `RefineSchedule::recursiveFillEdb` → `Schedule::communicate`.

The recovery path was calling `gridding_algorithm->regridAllFinerLevels()` manually after restoring the level-0 field. This direct invocation of the gridding algorithm outside the TimeRefinementIntegrator's normal regrid driver was crashing inside a SAMRAI MPI Schedule's communicate operation.

**Fix**: Removed the dangerous `removePatchLevel` + `regridAllFinerLevels` sequence. Instead:
1. Keep the level-0 field restoration (`restoreLevel0FromBuffer`) — real state load
2. Add a call to `refineLevel0ToHigherLevels` to overwrite fine-level cells with values from the corresponding coarse-level cells using injection refinement (this is the existing helper). This prevents stale fine-level data from contaminating the just-restored level-0 cells during the integrator's coarsen sync.
3. Let the TimeRefinementIntegrator do its own natural regrid on its normal schedule (every 2 steps by default).

This avoids the crash while still performing real state capture (level-0 field + metadata) and real state load (overwriting cell values from VeloC-restored buffer).
