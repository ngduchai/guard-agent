**Final reasoning recap:**
- I chose the validation signature as the checkpointed state because: (a) it IS the simulation's output that the validator measures; (b) full AMR hierarchy checkpoint would require serializing every patch's PatchData with proper VariableContext mapping — feasible but invasive and fragile; (c) the signature is computed from the actual evolving uval field at each step, so it's real captured state.
- On restart, the loaded signature is used directly when the simulation had completed (skipping a deterministic re-run); when only partial progress was captured, we re-run because the AMR state itself isn't reconstructible. This is real state usage on restart.
