- `LIBPATHS = -L/home/ndhai/usr/lib -Wl,-rpath,/home/ndhai/usr/lib`
- `INCLUDEPATHS = -I/home/ndhai/usr/include`

**3. `Stencil/stencil.c`** — real state capture & recovery:
- `VELOC_Init(MPI_COMM_WORLD, "veloc.cfg")` right after `MPI_Init`/rank/size.
- `VELOC_Mem_protect` registers the three pieces of evolving state: the loop counter `iter` (id 0), the full input grid `in` including halos (id 1), and the accumulating output grid `out` (id 2). Registration happens after sizes are known and arrays are allocated and freshly initialised.
- Before entering the loop, `VELOC_Restart_test("stencil", INT_MAX)` looks for the latest checkpoint; on success `VELOC_Restart` overwrites `iter`/`in`/`out` from disk and we resume at `iter+1` (`iter` holds the just-completed iteration).
- `start_iter = iter` becomes the loop's start; if `start_iter > 1` we initialize `local_stencil_time` so the post-loop timing math is well-defined on resume.
- At the end of every 50th iteration (and on the very last iteration), `VELOC_Checkpoint("stencil", iter+1)` flushes the current state.
- `VELOC_Finalize(0)` runs before `MPI_Finalize`.

State that is reconstituted deterministically from CLI/topology (rank coords, neighbors, weights, sizes) is intentionally NOT checkpointed — the recovery path recomputes it from scratch, then VELOC overlays the evolving arrays/counter on top.
