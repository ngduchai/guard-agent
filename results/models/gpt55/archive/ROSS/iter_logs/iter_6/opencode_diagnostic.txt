What changed:

- `models/phold/phold.main.c`
  - Moved initial GVT checkpoint trigger scheduling into `phold_ckpt_after_sched_init()`.
  - This hook runs from `tw_run()` after `setup_pes()` has created `g_tw_pe`.
  - Previously PHOLD called `tw_trigger_gvt_hook_at(...)` before `tw_run()`, but that function dereferences `g_tw_pe`, causing the immediate MPI abort seen by the validator.
  - Added a forward declaration for `phold_ckpt_gvt_hook`.
  - Increased `PHOLD_CKPT_PERIOD` from `500.0` to `2000.0` to reduce checkpoint overhead while still checkpointing well before the validator’s 50% kill floor.

- Existing real VeloC behavior remains in place:
  - `VELOC_Init(...)`
  - `VELOC_Mem_protect(...)`
  - `VELOC_Checkpoint(...)`
  - `VELOC_Restart_test(...)`
  - `VELOC_Restart(...)`
  - `VELOC_Finalize(...)`

- `veloc.cfg` is present and valid:
  - `scratch = /tmp/ross_veloc_scratch`
  - `persistent = /tmp/ross_veloc_persistent`
  - `mode = sync`

Why this addresses the rejection:

- The validator showed the resilient binary exited in about 1 second with empty app stdout/stderr and zero checkpoint files.
- The concrete crash path was scheduling the GVT hook before `g_tw_pe` existed.
- Now checkpoint scheduling happens only after scheduler/PE setup, so the run should reach the event loop, emit VeloC checkpoints, and be able to restore from them.
