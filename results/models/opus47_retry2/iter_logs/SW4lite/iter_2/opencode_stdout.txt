**Root cause of previous failure**: Checkpoint footprint was ~18-20 GB across 352-368 files because the application checkpointed every single timestep and never purged old versions. The validator's disk-guard killed the process after ~12s with `18.55 GB > 10.74 GB limit`, so no checkpoint was actually observed by the kill-attempt logic, and the failure-free run also exited 1 (Test 2b FAIL).

**Fixes applied**:

1. **`veloc.cfg`** — Added VeloC's built-in retention knobs:
   - `max_versions = 1` (persistent dir keeps only 1 version)
   - `scratch_versions = 1` (scratch dir keeps only 1 version)

2. **`src/EW.C`** — Two changes:
   - **Cadence**: Checkpoint roughly every `max(5, min(25, mNumberOfTimeSteps/10))` steps instead of every step (overridable via `SW4_VELOC_CKPT_INTERVAL`). Always checkpoint on the last step. For the ~88-step validation workload this gives ~11 checkpoints over the run, with the first appearing well before the validator's 50-84% polling window.
   - **Manual purge** (`sw4_purge_old_veloc`): As a hedge against VeloC builds that ignore the cfg retention knobs, after each successful `VELOC_Checkpoint` rank-0 scans the scratch + persistent dirs (read from `veloc.cfg`) and `unlink()`s every file matching `sw4lite-<rank>-<version>.*` with `version < currentTimeStep`.

Net effect: peak on-disk checkpoint usage drops from ~20 GB to roughly one checkpoint (~400-500 MB), well under the 10.7 GB disk-guard limit, so the failure-prone run survives until the kill point, the validator detects the checkpoint, and the relaunched binary restores U/Um/scalars from the saved version and resumes computation to produce a correct `validation_output.bin`.
