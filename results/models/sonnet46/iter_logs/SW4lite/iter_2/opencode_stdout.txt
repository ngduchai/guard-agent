## Summary of Changes

### Problem
The previous attempt failed because the app was checkpointing **every single timestep**, generating 360 checkpoint files (90 per rank) in just 12 seconds. This produced ~18 GB of checkpoint data, exceeding the 10.74 GB disk limit and causing termination before any checkpoint could be verified.

### Fixes Applied

**1. `veloc.cfg` — added `max_ckpts = 2`**
- VeloC was retaining every checkpoint version indefinitely
- With `max_ckpts = 2`, only the 2 most recent checkpoint versions are kept on disk
- This bounds disk usage to ~2 × checkpoint_size regardless of how many checkpoints are taken

**2. `src/EW.C` — checkpoint every 10 timesteps instead of every timestep**
- Changed the checkpoint call from unconditional to conditional: `if( currentTimeStep % 10 == 0 || currentTimeStep == mNumberOfTimeSteps )`
- The total run has ~810 timesteps; checkpointing every 10 gives 81 checkpoint opportunities
- With `max_ckpts=2`, only 2 versions are kept (~440 MB total vs 18 GB before)
- The validator waits until 50% of runtime (~54s) before polling for checkpoints — with a checkpoint every ~1.3s (10 steps × 0.13s/step), a checkpoint will easily be detected in time
- On restart after a kill at ~50% of runtime, the app resumes from the last checkpoint (within 10 steps before the kill), completing the remaining ~50% of steps — this gives a recovery ratio well under the 0.9 threshold needed
