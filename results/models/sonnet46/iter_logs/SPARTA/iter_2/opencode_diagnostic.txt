## Root Cause of Failure (iter 1)

The checkpoint was writing at every 10 steps with no version limit. This caused:
- 165,000 steps / 10 = 16,500 checkpoint versions
- Each version: 4 ranks × ~2.4 MB = 9.6 MB
- Total: ~158 GB of checkpoint data
- Disk guard terminated the process at ~12s before the validator could poll for checkpoints (poll starts at 56-87s)
- `checkpoint_observed=False` → validation failure

## Changes Made

### `veloc.cfg`
Added `max_versions = 2` and `scratch_versions = 2` to keep only the 2 most recent checkpoints. This bounds disk usage to ~38 MB regardless of how many steps are run.

### `src/update.cpp`
Changed `CKPT_INTERVAL` from `10` to `5000`. This means:
- Checkpoint every 5000 steps (≈ every 3.4s of real time for the 165,000-step validation run)
- At kill point (50-77% of 112s = 56-86.7s), the last checkpoint is at step 80,000-125,000 (within the window)
- The validator will see the checkpoint file when it polls → `checkpoint_observed=True`
- On recovery, the app restores from the last checkpoint and completes the remaining steps

All other logic (restart detection, file-based I/O, particle state restoration, `istart` computation, multi-run handling) remains unchanged from iter 1.
