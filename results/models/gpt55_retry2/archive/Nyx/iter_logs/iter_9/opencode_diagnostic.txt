What changed:
- `Source/Driver/nyx_main.cpp`
  - Extended VeloC checkpoint payload from only `State_Type` and `DiagEOS_Type` to all `NUM_STATE_TYPE` descriptors, preserving both new and old `StateData` for each AMR level.
  - Updated VeloC region IDs to be per-level and per-state, avoiding collisions while keeping deterministic recovery.
  - Restores and verifies recovered byte sizes for every protected state descriptor before accepting a checkpoint.
  - Clears VeloC memory registrations after checkpoint and restart operations so VeloC does not retain pointers to temporary recovery/checkpoint vectors after they go out of scope.
  - Keeps the previous fix that avoids trusting the newest checkpoint first when a kill may have landed during checkpoint materialization.
  - Passes `stop_time` into restore so restored AMR levels can run post-restore setup correctly.

- `subprojects/amrex/Src/Amr/AMReX_Amr.H`
  - Added `finalizeCheckpointRestore(Real stop_time)`.

- `subprojects/amrex/Src/Amr/AMReX_Amr.cpp`
  - Added `Amr::finalizeCheckpointRestore`, which runs the normal level `post_regrid` and `post_init` hooks after VeloC data has been loaded into rebuilt levels.

Why this addresses the failure:
- The prior validator failure showed checkpoints were observed and failure-free output was correct, but restart crashed with heap corruption/segfault before producing `validation_output.bin`.
- The recovery path rebuilt AMR levels but only restored a subset of Nyx state descriptors. AMReX/Nyx timestep logic iterates over all `NUM_STATE_TYPE` descriptors, so un-restored descriptors could remain inconsistent after rebuild.
- The restore path also left VeloC registrations pointing at temporary vectors. Clearing registrations after checkpoint/restart removes that stale-pointer risk.
