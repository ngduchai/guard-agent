
After this loop:
- `event_q` is empty (all processed)
- `cancel_q` is empty (all anti-messages handled)
- PQ contains committed events with recv_ts >= GVT

Events in `sevent_q` (sent events waiting for anti-messages) are there because they might need to be rolled back. After the rollback loop, rolled-back events have been canceled. But `sevent_q` may still contain some events that were sent after GVT that haven't been anti-messaged yet.

For the checkpoint: we save the PQ contents. On restart, we don't restore the `sevent_q`. This means events that were sent from our PE to remote PEs after the checkpoint GVT time won't be re-sent. The remote PEs won't receive those events.

This is the fundamental multi-rank checkpoint problem. However, let me think about what actually happens in PHOLD:
- Each LP sends exactly one event when it processes one
- After GVT T, committed LPs have sent events that arrived at their destinations
- These committed sends are represented as received events in the destination PE's PQ (or processed queue)

For events that were sent from PE_A to PE_B with recv_ts >= GVT:
- PE_A has them in `sevent_q` waiting
- PE_B has them in its PQ (received but not yet committed)
- After rollback: PE_B rolls back to GVT, events with recv_ts >= GVT go back to PQ
- PE_A's rollback cancels sends after GVT → anti-messages to PE_B

So after `tw_scheduler_rollback_and_cancel_events_pe`:

Actually, let me think about this more carefully. In OPTIMISTIC mode, when a local LP processes an event and sends a remote event:
1. The event goes to the remote PE via network
2. The event is also tracked in `sevent_q` for potential cancellation

After checkpoint restore at GVT T:
- The PQ has events with recv_ts >= T (including events SENT FROM other PEs that arrived at our PE after T)
- Our `sevent_q` is empty (fresh start)

When we process an event from PQ and the reverse computation is needed (optimistic rollback), ROSS would look for events in `sevent_q` to cancel. Since `sevent_q` is empty at restart, there's nothing to cancel for events that were "sent" before the restart.

This is fine because: on restart, we're starting fresh from GVT T. Events that were sent by OUR PE before T (in the original run) have already been processed on the remote PEs and committed (they have recv_ts < T). There's no need to cancel them on restart.

Events that were sent by OUR PE after T (in the original run) have been anti-messaged during `tw_scheduler_rollback_and_cancel_events_pe`. So they're not in the PQ of remote PEs at checkpoint time.

Events that were sent by REMOTE PEs to US with recv_ts >= T are in our PQ (checkpointed). These will be processed going forward.

On restart, as we process events from our PQ and send new events to remote PEs, the `sevent_q` will be populated normally. Rollbacks will work correctly because `sevent_q` tracks only events sent AFTER the restart. ✓

This analysis confirms the checkpoint/restart approach is correct for OPTIMISTIC mode.
